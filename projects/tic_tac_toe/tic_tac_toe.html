So, how does it work?

Alpha-Beta search is an algorithm used to help computers make decisions in two-player games, like chess or tic-tac-toe. Imagine you're playing a game, and for each turn, you need to choose the best move. You'd look at all the possible moves and their outcomes to decide which one is best.

The algorithm does the same thing by exploring a decision tree, which is like a map of all possible moves and their consequences. However, instead of examining every single move, Alpha-Beta search saves time by ignoring paths that clearly won't lead to a better outcome. This way, the computer can find the best move more quickly and efficiently.

Basically, Alpha-Beta search is so efficient at finding the best moves, it's like the computer version of an online-dating expert who can quickly swipe left on bad matches and choose a perfect date without breaking too much sweat!
